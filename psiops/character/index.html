    Your Character¶

    You’re a newly recruited PsiOps special forces operative: a steely-eyed, door-kicking professional who uses military hardware and doctrines as efficiently as you can unleash the paranormal abilities of your mind. You will live a dangerous life. You will drop into weird and hostile combat zones. With some luck, you might retire before dying.

    Step 1: Name, Appearance, and Backstory¶

    Give your character a name and describe how they look. Choose one or two physical characteristics that people first notice.

    Create a little backstory for your character. You chose to become a soldier for a reason—what is it?

    Also, think about your character’s relationship with the other soldiers in your squad, but you don’t have to decide all this now; developing your character as you play is okay. Don’t get too attached to your character; it’s a dangerous and uncertain world.

    Step 2: Drive¶

    Next, pick one trait from each of the following three pairs that best describes your character’s outlook on life, personality, and motivation—collectively called your drive.

    These pairs form triads of Bright (empathy, protection, selflessness) and Dark (callousness, manipulation, self-entitlement) traits of a character’s personality.

    For example, a character with all bright traits would be the traditional hero. Conversely, a character with all dark traits is quite the villain. A mix is somewhere between those extremes.

    Traits Table

    Bright Dark
    1 Empathetic or Callous
    2 Protective or Manipulative
    3 Selfless or Self-Entitled

    These traits set a tone for role-playing your character, letting others know what to expect.

    Trait Combination Descriptions

    BBB: The Hero. An everyday saint. Someone who is loved by all and who, in turn, spends their life trying to make the world better for everyone, no matter the stakes or the personal reward.

    BBD: The Mercenary. A fairly good-hearted person who will fight to protect the weak as long as they get something in return.

    BDB: The Parent. Someone who genuinely cares about and feels deeply for others but is okay with deceiving and manipulating them, as long as it’s for the other’s well-being.

    DBB: The Reluctant Hero. A gritty, down-to-earth type who may appear gruff and cold, even rude on the outside, but who, deep down, truly cares for their friends.

    DDB: The Tribal Leader. Someone who cares for their people but has little to no regard for outsiders. They are willing to do anything for themselves and their people, no matter the cost to outsiders.

    DBD: The Guard. A merciless protector of the status quo, who cares only for the pay they receive, but who won’t tolerate fighting or exploitation amongst those under their charge.

    BDD: The Intelligencer. An expert manipulator who can find and exploit people’s strengths and weaknesses, connecting with them emotionally only to use them.

    DDD: The Villain. An antisocial, cruel, greedy person who cares only for personal gain and will betray, deceive, or steal to get what they want.

    These starting traits may change as you adventure and further develop your character.

    Step 3: Attribute Scores¶

    Three attributes describe your character’s primary characteristics:

    • STRENGTH (STR): brawn, endurance, toughness
    • DEXTERITY (DEX): agility, finesse, quickness
    • WILLPOWER (WIL): charisma, command, focus

    Is your character musclebound and insightful? Nimble and charming? Strength and Dexterity are your physical attributes; Willpower is your mental attribute. Attribute scores define these qualities—your character’s assets and weaknesses.

    Roll 1D6+4 three times, noting the results. Assign the highest value to Willpower and the other two as you prefer to your Strength and Dexterity.

    Note: If you prefer not to use psionic abilities, you do not need to assign the highest value to your Willpower; set all three attribute scores as you see fit.

    3.1 Dice Modifiers¶

    Your attribute score generates Dice Modifiers (DM) as follows.
    Attribute 0 1–2 3–6 7–9 10-11 12
    Modifier -2 -1 0 +1 +2 +3

    In this document, the attribute, when written out, such as Strength, refers to the attribute score, whereas the abbreviation, such as STR, refers to the DM.

    Step 4: Hit Points¶

    Hit points (HP) represent the damage you can shrug off before suffering any actual harm. HP is easily and quickly restored.

    Your starting hit points are equal to your Strength plus Dexterity divided by two: (Strength + Dexterity)/2. Drop any fractions.

    Step 5: Skills and Training¶

    In addition to attribute scores, your character is also defined by the skills they learn during their military career. Skills represent areas of specialized knowledge or proficiency. Skills are rated from levels zero to three, with level zero representing a basic understanding, level one competence, level two increasing experience and knowledge, and level three mastery.

    You have six training opportunities: civilian, basic, advanced, general soldiering, PsiOps, and personal. At each stage, you learn new skills or improve existing skills. We’ll step through each stage one at a time.

    See the Skills section for descriptions of each skill.

    5.1 Civilian Life¶

    Before joining the military, you gained some background skills in your civilian life. What was your former occupation?

    Roll on the following table or create a new one with a relevant skill.

    All civilians start with Wheeled Vehicle-0 and an occupation-related skill.

    d6 Former Occupation Skill

    5.2: Basic Training¶

    Your basic military training after enlisting provided you with the following skills:

    • Gun Combat (Pistol-0, Rifle-0, Shotgun-0)
    • Heavy Weapons-0
    • Machinegun-0
    • Melee Combat-0
    • +1 to the lower score of either Strength or Dexterity (note that this increase may also change your HP)

    5.5: PsiOps Special Forces Training¶

    After joining PsiOps, you learned the following skills:

    • airborne operations (parachute-0)
    • artic operations (skiing-0)
    • underwater operations (subaqua-0)

    If you already have any of these three skills, increase the skill by one.

    You also trained in a specific squad combat role. Pick one of the squad roles in the following table and gain the associated level-1 skill and equipment. Each squad can have only one of each combat role, so the players should coordinate their selections. Also, each squad must have either a squad or a team leader role.

    If you already have the specialized skill, increase the skill by two to a maximum of level-3.

    5.6: Personal Training¶

    Finally, add one point to either an existing skill or one of your attribute scores.

    Step 6: Psionic Training¶

    As a soldier identified to possess latent psionic abilities, you underwent training after joining PsiOps to manifest these paranormal talents.

    If Willpower is your highest attribute, you may attempt to learn psionic talents by making a training check during character creation. Talents are checked in the following order:

    1. Telepathy: reading minds and mental communication
    2. Extra Sensory Perception (ESP): perceiving at a distance
    3. Telekinesis: mind over matter
    4. Awareness: control over one's mind and body
    5. Teleportation: moving from one point to another instantly

    The number of dice in your psionics training dice pool equals your WIL+4. Roll all the dice in the pool. You learn the talent if you roll a 5 or 6 on any die. If the highest die is a 4 or less, you fail to learn it, and that talent is unavailable—go on to the next one. After each check, remove one die from the dice pool before trying to learn the next talent. If all dice are removed from your pool, you can’t attempt to learn any remaining talents.

    If none of the five talents are learned, psionic abilities are unavailable to your character. However, your character does possess a latent ability to shield against telepathic readings of their surface thoughts. Also, any checks your character makes against psionic attacks have an advantage (+1DM). Your mind is inherently resistant to psionics, though unable to manifest the discrete talents.

    For now, make a note of the psionic abilities you’ve unlocked. The Psionics section details these talents.

    Step 7: Introductions¶

    Finally, create a short description of your character, preferably a sentence or so, and introduce your character to the other players in your squad.

    Good luck, soldier!
