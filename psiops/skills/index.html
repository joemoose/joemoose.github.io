    Skill Descriptions¶

    Combat¶

    Archery

    The character is skilled at using and maintaining bows and crossbows for hunting or combat.

    Heavy Weapons

    The Heavy Weapons skill covers man-portable and larger weapons that cause extreme property damage, such as rocket launchers and artillery.

    Melee Combat

    The various specialties of this skill cover different types of personal melee combat weapons.

    Pistol

    The character is skilled at using and maintaining pistols.

    Rifle

    The character is skilled at using and maintaining assault rifles.

    Shotgun

    The character is skilled at using and maintaining shotguns.

    Field¶

    Athletics

    This skill covers physical fitness and training, similar to a trained athlete’s. This includes acts requiring physical coordination, such as climbing, juggling, or throwing; acts of endurance, such as long-distance running or hiking; and acts of strength, such as weight-lifting or bodybuilding.

    Comms

    The character is trained in using, repairing, and maintaining communications and sensor devices. While anyone can press the button and make a communicator function, this skill is necessary to understand why the device does not work or how to use the device for purposes other than open transmission. Skilled characters can boost an incoming or outgoing signal, create or break a secure channel, detect signals and anomalies, hide or piggyback on another signal, jam local communications, locate and assess potential threats, and analyze complex sensor data.

    Computer

    The character is skilled in programming and operating computer systems. Anyone with Computer-0 or better can perform the following without a skill check: Log on to a Datanet, send and receive messages, search for non-classified information, and retrieve data and files. More skilled users can create or break data encryption; mine data effectively; create or break data and network security protocols; and perform other general programming tasks.

    Demolitions

    This skill covers demolition charges and other explosive devices, including assembling or disarming bombs.

    Electronics

    The character is practiced in installing, using, maintaining, repairing, and creating electronic devices and equipment. The character knows how to use the tools of the trade, perform routine tasks, supervise untrained helpers, and handle common problems.

    The character is skilled at disarming alarm systems, picking electronic locks, disabling a robotic device, or similar tasks involving electronics.

    Engineering

    The character is skilled in the large-scale construction or repair of buildings, bridges, fortifications, water supplies, electrical power grids, etc. The prepared engineer can work with a team of workers, raw materials, and construction equipment to build structures.

    Jack-of-All-Trades (JoT)

    The JoT skill works differently from other skills. It adds the JoT level as DM to another player’s checks when the player with JoT assists. JoT also removes the -1 DM for attempting a task without the associated skill.

    Mechanics

    The character is practiced in installing, using, maintaining, repairing, and creating engines and mechanical devices. The character knows how to use the tools of the trade, perform routine tasks, supervise untrained helpers, and handle common problems.

    The character is skilled at disabling mechanical alarm systems, picking mechanical locks, or similar tasks involving mechanical devices.

    Medicine

    The individual has training and skill in the medical arts and sciences, from diagnosis and triage to surgery and other corrective treatments. This skill represents a character's ability to provide emergency, short-term, long-term, and specialized treatment for diseases, poisons, and debilitating injuries.

    Navigation

    The character is trained in navigation, plotting positions on charts, using a compass to chart a route, determining a heading, estimating arrival times, and more. The modern military navigator often uses a GPS locator. The navigator’s map skills allow him to coordinate the firepower of artillery, aircraft, and even ships off-shore to assist in the squad’s tactical goals. They can call in coordinates on the radio and illuminate targets using a laser designator. The more expert they become, the greater the firepower they can coordinate.

    Recon

    This skill represents the ability to scout out dangers and spot threats, unusual objects, or out-of-place people. Characters skilled in Recon are adept at staying unseen and unheard.

    Riding

    This skill grants the ability to maneuver and care for horses properly.

    Survival

    The character is skilled in the art of survival in the wild, including hunting or trapping animals, avoiding exposure, locating sources of food and fresh water (if available), producing fires (where possible), finding shelter, avoiding dangerous flora and fauna, avoiding getting lost, and dealing with the dangers of hazardous climates (arctic, desert, etc.).

    Tactics

    This skill covers tactical planning and decision-making, from board games to squad-level combat to large-scale engagements.

    Science¶

    Life Sciences

    This skill represents theoretical and practical knowledge derived from the scientific study of living organisms. This covers various related fields, such as biochemistry, biology, botany, genetics, and physiology.

    Physical Sciences

    This skill represents theoretical and practical knowledge derived from the scientific study of the nature and properties of energy and non-living matter. This covers various related fields, such as chemistry, electronics, geology, and physics.

    Social Sciences

    This skill represents theoretical and practical knowledge derived from the scientific study of sophont society and social relationships. This covers various related fields, such as archeology, economics, history, philosophy, and psychology.

    Social¶

    Admin

    The character has experience with bureaucratic agencies and understands the requirements of dealing with and managing them. When attempting tasks like avoiding police harassment, ensuring the prompt issuance of licenses, approval of applications, avoiding close inspection of papers, etc.

    Advocate

    The character is familiar with the general laws and regulations that govern travel, commerce, and relations. This skill does not impart knowledge of the various laws of each nation nor allows the person to act as an attorney.

    Bribery

    The character has experience bribing petty and not-so-petty officials to circumvent regulations or ignore cumbersome laws.

    Broker

    A broker is skilled in locating suppliers and buyers and facilitating the purchase and resale of commercial goods.

    Carousing

    This skill reflects the interpersonal art of interacting and socializing with others. The character is skilled in the art of small talk and making others feel at ease in their presence in almost any social situation, such as a party, ball, inauguration, bar hopping, etc.

    Gambling

    The individual is well informed on games of chance and wise in their play. They have an advantage over non-experts and can generally win when engaged in such games. Gambling, however, should not be confused with general risk-taking.

    Leadership

    The character can rally, inspire, organize, and direct team efforts to ensure the best cooperation and productivity possible.

    Linguistics

    The character can read and write a different language for every level of Linguistics they possess. All characters can speak and read their native language without needing Linguistics skill, and automated computer translator programs mean that Linguistics skill is not always needed. Having Linguistics-0 implies that the character has many simple phrases in many languages. In addition, Linguistics can be used to attempt to decipher the general meaning of a preserved specimen of language, such as an inscription or a recorded message.

    Liaison

    The character is trained in dealing with others, including knowledge of proper protocols, manners of address, codes of conduct, and other information needed when dealing with a wide range of societal types. Such a character is helpful when attempting to negotiate a particularly edgy deal, convince the tribal leader to assist the team, help settle a dispute between two opposing groups, or perform other acts of negotiation and diplomacy.

    Steward

    The Steward skill allows the character to serve and care for troops or (in civilian life) customers. This includes knowledge of concierge duties, housekeeping services, meal preparation, and presentation, personal grooming assistance and valet service, and proper social etiquette.

    Streetwise

    A character with the Streetwise skill understands the urban environment and the power structures in society. A skilled character knows where to go for information, how to handle strangers without offending them, and who can handle activities bordering on the fringe of legality.

    Transportation¶

    Motorboats

    This skill grants the ability to maneuver and perform routine maintenance on small motorized watercraft properly.

    Ocean Ships

    This skill grants the ability to properly maneuver and perform routine maintenance on large motorized sea-going ships.

    Parachute

    The skill and practice of jumping from an airplane and landing with a static-line or steerable parachute. Includes high altitude, low opening (HALO), and high altitude, high opening techniques (HAHO).

    Rotary Aircraft

    This skill grants the ability to properly maneuver and perform essential, routine maintenance on helicopters, hovercraft, and other similar craft. Rotor aircraft can hover but require skill checks to keep steady in adverse environmental conditions.

    Skiing

    The skill and practice of cross-country skiing and arctic operations.

    Subaqua

    The skill and practice of using a self-contained underwater breathing apparatus (SCUBA), combat swimming, and underwater operations.

    Submarine

    This skill grants the ability to properly maneuver and perform essential, routine maintenance on underwater vehicles.

    Tracked Vehicle

    This skill grants the ability to properly maneuver and perform essential, routine maintenance on tanks and other vehicles that move on tracks.

    Wheeled Vehicle

    This skill grants the ability to properly maneuver and perform routine maintenance on automobiles and similar wheeled vehicles.

    Winged Aircraft

    This skill grants the ability to properly maneuver and perform essential, routine maintenance on jets and other airplanes using a lifting body.
