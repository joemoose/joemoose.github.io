    Scholar¶

    A scholar of infinite knowledge who plunders the pages of lost tomes and finds forgotten treasures in the scripts of ancient peoples, now lost in the mists of time—that’s you!

    Stats¶

    Stat Value
    Base damage d6
    Load 8+STR
    Maximum HP 6+Constitution

    Heritage¶

    Heritage Move
    Dwarf You can also count the dwarves and their mountains as part of your scholarly lore.
    Elf You can also count the elves and their forests as part of your scholarly lore.
    Halfling You can also count the halflings and their shires as part of your scholarly lore.
    Human Pick a second area of expertise for your starting scholarly lore.

    Drives¶

    • Give someone the advice they need to change their life.
    • Leave someone more knowledgeable than when you met them.
    • Support the lawful order with your wisdom.
    • Uncover a secret truth.

    Gear¶

    You start with scholarly robes, a bag of books (5 uses, 2 weight), and dungeon rations (5 uses, 1 weight).

    Choose two of the following:

    • adventuring gear (5 uses, 1 weight),
    • antitoxin and bandages (3 uses, slow)
    • Halfling pipeleaf (6 uses)
    • leather armor (1 armor, 1 weight)
    • staff (reach, 2 weight)
    • worn bow (near, 2 weight) and a bundle of arrows (3 ammo, 1 weight).

    Starting Moves¶

    Move Description
    Consult Scholar When another PC comes to you for advice about something important, and you tell them what you think is best, they gain +1 forward when following your advice. At the end of a session, if any other PCs benefited from following your advice, you can mark XP.
    I Know This Work When you encounter an interesting artifact, construction, or device (your call), roll +WIS. On a hit, you can ask the GM questions. On a 10+, ask three. On a 7-9, ask two:
  1. How could I make this thing do _____?
  2. How is it weak or vulnerable?
  3. What’s happened to it recently?
  4. What’s it for?
  5. What’s it made of?
  6. Who made it?

    7. On a miss, it is something beyond your knowledge and understanding.
    Scholarly Lore Choose an area of expertise:
  7. A Bestiary of Creatures Unusual
  8. The Dead and Undead
  9. Gods and their Servants
  10. Grand Histories of the Known World
  11. Legends of Heroes Past
  12. The Planar Spheres
  13. Spells and Magicks

    14. When you first encounter an important creature, location, or item (your call) covered by your scholarly lore, you can ask the GM any one question about it; the GM will answer truthfully.

    The GM may then ask you what legend, song, or tale you heard that information in.

    Advanced Moves¶

    Move Description
    Assistance When you successfully aid someone, you gain +1 forward as well.
    Eloquent When you parley with someone, you also gain +1 forward with them on a hit.
    Fount of Knowledge When you spout lore about something no one else knows, gain +1 forward.
    Quick Study When you see the effects of an arcane spell, ask the GM the name of the spell and its effects. You gain +1 forward when acting on the answers.
    Research Assistant You have a trusted assistant or partner who supports you in everything you do. Describe them and give them a name.

    They count as a hireling with loyalty +2 and skill points equal to your level+1, with your choice of skills, plus one of the following abilities:
  14. When they help you defend, you gain +1 hold.
  15. When they help you defy danger, gain +1 forward.
  16. When they help you fight, you deal +d4 damage.
  17. When they help you parley, gain +1 forward.

    18. They have no HP. When they take damage, lower your own HP. If you die or refuse Death’s bargain, your buddy can sacrifice their own life so that you may live.
    Sabbatical When you return to a civilized settlement you've visited before, tell the GM when you were last here. They'll let you know how it's changed since then. Additionally, at your option, name a person you met here or something you left behind.
    Scholarly Expert Requires Scholarly Lore
    Choose another area of expertise from the lore list.

    Master Moves¶

    Move Description
    Book of Names When you meet someone important (your call), say what you’ve heard about them and roll +WIS. On a hit, the GM will tell you more details. On a 10+, the GM’s details will complement yours. On a 7-9, the GM’s details may contradict your information, and how you discover the truth is your affair. On a miss, you are wrong about something important.
    An Ear for Magic When you hear an enemy cast a spell, the GM will tell you the spell's name and its effects. Gain +1 forward when acting on the answers.
    Evidence of Faith When you see divine magic as it happens, you can ask the GM which deity granted the spell and its effects. Gain +1 when acting on the answers.
    Graduate Studies Requires Research Assistant
    Give your trusted assistant or partner a second ability from the list.
    Noted Research When you meet someone you've met before (your call) after some time apart, you gain +1 forward against them.
    Persuasive Requires Eloquent
    When you parley with a player character, on a hit, you gain +1 forward with them and can ask their player one question, which they must answer truthfully.
    Reputation When you first meet someone who’s heard of you, roll +CHA. On a 10+, tell the GM two things they’ve heard about you. On a 7-9, tell the GM one thing they’ve heard, and the GM will tell you one thing.
    Wealth of Knowledge When you spout lore, on a 12+, you ask the GM a question, but the GM will also ask you a question about the subject. Whatever you answer, it is the truth.
