    Templars¶

    Your Martyred Saint called on you for a reason. You hear her voice in your dreams and see her in the eyes of the faithless. You sense her guiding hand when your enemies loom.

    They brand you with false names: fiend, inquisitor, zealot. These insults are not who you are. You are a Templar of the Martyred Saint. One who endures in her name.

    Your will is iron, your cause just, and your foes doomed.

    Stats¶

    Stat Value
    Base Damage d8
    Load 10+STR
    Maximum HP 10+Constitution

    Diety¶

    You serve and worship the Martyred Saint. She speaks to and through you. She represents:

    • Mercy for the oppressed.
    • Wrath against slavery of body, mind, or soul, whether of the living or the dead.
    • The sanctity of suffering and is petitioned by such.

    The Martyred Saint has multiple personas manifest in different situations: Healing, Protection, and Wisdom through her Disciples; Judgement through her Monks; Death through her Incarnations. Vengence through her Undying; And Battle through you, her Templar.

    She has a favored group, a chosen people, the followers of the Saint.

    Heritage¶

    Heritage Move
    Dwarf Choose one:
    • When you roll for I Am the Law, on a hit, you also gain +d4 damage forward against those you are ordering around.
    • When you take the damage meant for someone else, you gain +1 armor.
    Elf Choose one:
    • Choose one weapon—you can always treat weapons of that type as if they had the precise tag.
    • When you Discern Reality while protecting someone, you can also ask, “How can I get my ward out of here?”
    Halfling Choose one:
    • When you attack with a ranged weapon, deal +2 damage.
    • When you Defy Danger and use your small size to your advantage, gain +1.
    Human Choose one:
    • When you pray for guidance, even for a moment, and ask, “What here is antithetical to the Martyred Saint?” the GM will tell you.
    • When your flock or hirelings helps you fight, you deal +d4 damage.

    Drives¶

    • Deny mercy to a criminal or unbeliever.
    • Endanger yourself to combat an unnatural threat.
    • Endanger yourself to protect someone weaker than you.

    Gear¶

    You start with all the following:

    • A symbol of the Martyred Saint (0 weight, describe it)
    • Dungeon Rations (5 uses, 1 weight)
    • Adventuring Gear (5 uses, 1 weight)
    • A longsword, axe, or mace (close, 1 weight)
    • A tabard (+1 armor, 1 weight)

    You also start with one of the following:

    • A greatsword, maul, or executioner’s axe (reach, +1 damage, two-handed, 2 weight)
    • A crossbow (near, far, 2 weight)

    Finally, choose one:

    • Armoured Breastplate (+1 armor, clumsy, 1 weight)
    • Poultices and herbs (2 uses, slow, 1 weight) and antitoxin

    Starting Moves¶

    Move Description
    Battle Miracles At the start of a session or when you eliminate a powerful enemy antithetical to the Martyred Saint, gain one of the following miracles of the Martyred Saint:
    • Bless
    • Magic Weapon
    • Wisdom of War
    When you invoke the power of this miracle, roll +WIS.
    • On a 7+, the miracle manifests, but you can’t invoke the miracle again until after you sleep for a night.
    • On a 10+, the miracle’s power is retained, and you may invoke it again.
    Law When you give an NPC an order based on your divine authority, roll +CHA.
    • On a 7+, they choose one:
      • Do what you say
      • Back away cautiously, then flee
      • Attack you
    • On a 10+, you also gain +1 forward against them.
    • On a miss, they do as they please, and you suffer -1 forward against them.
    Squire You have an apprentice templar. Name and describe them. Your squire’s skill for all their abilities equals a third of your level (rounding up.)

    They start with the following abilities:
    • Intervene: When your squire defies danger alongside you, you may roll +skill instead. If you do, the squire takes the brunt of any consequences.
    • Ministry: When you make camp with your squire, if you would heal, you also recover +skill HP.
    During a session, the GM may ask what lessons you’ve taught your squire about the faith. If you answer, mark XP.

    Advanced Moves¶

    Move Description
    Authority Gain +1 to order hirelings.
    Behold When you invoke your miracles in battle, you gain +1 to the roll.
    Bound When you and your squire deal damage together, add 2x skill to the damage. If there are consequences to your actions, you and the squire share them.
    Charge! When you lead the charge into combat, those you lead gain +1 forward.
    Command When you command a group of willing NPCs to do something, they do it, but the GM picks one or more from the following:
    • It’ll take days/weeks/months to complete
    • The flock will question their faith, or yours
    • Doing so will leave _____ vulnerable
    • It will cost you _____
    • First, you must _____
    • You’ll need help from _____
    • You must first defeat _____
    • The best they can manage is a lesser version
    Protector When you defend the unarmed or defenseless, gain a +1 hold, even on a miss.

    Master Moves¶

    Move Description
    Crusader Requires Command
    When you Lead the Flock in a battle against enemies of the Martyred Saint, they will always give their all for the cause. The GM may not choose “the flock will question their faith” or “the best they can manage is a lesser version.”
    High Warden Replaces Protector
    When you defend, you always gain a +1 hold, even on a miss. When you get a 12+ to defend, the nearest creature is distracted or momentarily stunned, granting you a clear opportunity.
    Divine Authority Replaces Authority
    Gain +1 ongoing when ordering hirelings. When you roll a 12+, the hireling transcends their moment of fear and doubt and carries out your order with particular effectiveness or efficiency.
    Greater Battle Miracles Requires Battle Miracles
    Add the following Battle Miracles to your list.
    • Fireball
    • Immunity
    Onward Replaces Charge!
    When you lead the charge into combat, those you lead gain +1 forward and +2 armor forward.

    Battle Miracles¶

    Miracle Description
    Bless Ongoing
    The Martyred Saint smiles on you or an ally of your choice. Gain +1 ongoing so long as the battle and spell continue. While this spell is ongoing, you take -1 to cast another spell.
    Fireball You evoke a mighty flame that envelops your target and everyone nearby, inflicting 2d6 damage that ignores armor.
    Immunity Ongoing
    Name one specific source of harm, such as burning, cutting, lightning, or poisonous gas. While this spell is ongoing, you are immune to harm from that source and take -1 to cast another spell. You may still be subject to secondary effects—a fire that does not burn you produces smoke that can still choke you, and drowning is dreadfully uncomfortable.
    Magic Weapon Ongoing
    The weapon you hold while casting does +d4 damage until you dismiss this spell. While this spell is ongoing, you take -1 to cast another spell.
    Wisdom of War When cast during a battle or on the spot of a historical battle, the GM will tell you something interesting and helpful about that battle, as if you were Spouting Lore and rolled a 10+.
