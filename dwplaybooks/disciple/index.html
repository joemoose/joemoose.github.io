    Disciple of the Martyred Saint¶

    You have been imbued with divine abilities to reward your faith in the Martyred Saint—The One Who Endures. These abilities take the form of divine spells.

    Your path in life is to face danger head-on and defy it. What better way to show your virtue to the Martyred Saint and her teachings than to suffer and spread her miracles?

    Stats¶

    Stat Value
    Base damage d6
    Load 9+STR
    Maximum HP 8+Constitution

    Diety¶

    You serve and worship the Martyred Saint. She speaks to and through you. She represents:

    • Mercy for the oppressed.
    • Wrath against slavery of body, mind, or soul, whether of the living or the dead.
    • The sanctity of suffering and is petitioned by such.

    The Martyred Saint has multiple personas manifest in different situations: Battle through her Templars; Judgement through her Monks; Death through her Incarnations; Vengence through her Undying; And Healing, Protection, and Wisdom through you, her Disciple.

    She has a favored group, a chosen people, the followers of the Saint.

    Heritage¶

    Heritage Move
    Dwarf Choose one:
    • When you suffer a debility, you may take an additional d4 damage (ignores armor) and gain +1 forward.
    • You are one with stone. When you commune, you are also granted a particular version of the level five spell Words of the Unspeaking as a rote that works only on stone.
    Elf Choose one:
    • You are always graceful, even in suffering. When you take a debility to DEX, you suffer a -1 penalty to another stat of your choice, but never to DEX.
    • You know what patience is. For you, non-violent protest counts as making camp and recovering, even under harsh conditions.
    Halfling Choose one:
    • You sing the Healing Songs of Spring and Brook. When you make camp, you and your allies heal +d6.
    • When you take a debility, you gain +DEX armor.
    Human Choose one:
    • When you pray for guidance, even for a moment, if you ask, “What here is antithetical to the Martyred Saint?” the GM will tell you, honestly.
    • You can have two debilities affecting your WIS score. Each gives you a -1 penalty, and having both gives you a -2 penalty. This brings your maximum number of debilities up to seven.

    Drives¶

    • Bring the benefits of the Saint to people of a different faith.
    • Defend others who share your faith.
    • Discredit a dangerous misconception about your sphere influence.
    • Disrupt the workings of authorities who do not share your faith.
    • Sacrifice something to your sphere of influence instead of accepting personal gain.

    Gear¶

    You have clothes appropriate to your calling, dungeon rations (5 uses, 1 weight), and an item that symbolizes your devotion to the Martyred Saint (0 weight, describe it), and choose one:

    • Adventuring gear (5 uses, 1 weight)
    • Bandages (3 uses, slow) and a healing potion
    • Staff (close, two-handed, 1 weight)

    Starting Moves¶

    Move Description
    Blessed You are devoted to the Martyred Saint. She fills your life with purpose, your soul with enthusiasm, and your limbs with strength.

    Your Devotion to the Martyred Saint gives you a unique insight and the ability to cast spells in the following spheres of her divine power:
    • Healing
    • Protection
    • Wisdom
    Also, as a disciple of the Martyred Saint, you live by a set of codes, strictures, or tenets, things you are supposed to do, and some things you are forbidden to do.

    At a minimum, the Saint’s teachings demand the following personal code:
    • Must ease suffering whenever you can.
    • Forbidden from wearing armor.
    • Harming others requires penance: fasting or self-flagellation.
    You can also further define your personal code during play.
    Cast a Divine Spell When you unleash a spell granted to you by your devotion, roll+WIS.
    • On a 10+, the spell is successfully cast, and your devotion does not revoke it; you may cast it again.
    • On a 7-9, the spell is cast, but choose one:
      • After you cast it, the spell is revoked. You can only cast the spell again once you Commune and have it granted to you.
      • You draw unwelcome attention or put yourself in a spot. The GM will tell you how.
      • Your casting strains your devotion—take -1 ongoing to cast a spell until the next time you commune.
    Note: Maintaining spells with ongoing effects will sometimes cause a penalty to your roll to cast a spell.
    Commune When you spend uninterrupted time in quiet contemplation of your devotion (an hour or so), you:
    • Lose any spells already granted to you.
    • Are granted new spells of your choice whose total levels don’t exceed your level+1, none of which is higher than your level.
    • Prepare all of your rotes, which never count against your limit.
    Your spheres of divine insight determine which spells you can cast. See the following Spells section. You cannot be granted spells from other divine spheres or classes.
    Sacrifice When you touch someone and make a demand of them, if they do what you ask, take an attribute debility of your choice and choose one:
    • They are healed for half your current HP.
    • They are healed of one debility.
    • They are healed of one injury (like a broken arm).
    • They gain d8 temporary HP. Damage reduced the temporary HP first, but it cannot be healed.
    You must ask for something immediate, not long-term, and if you already have all six debilities, you cannot heal them.
    Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken When you prepare yourself to endure some hardship without fighting back, roll+CON.
    • On a hit, you can take half damage or completely ignore another ill effect.
    • On a 10+, you can defeat, expose, or frustrate your oppressor with your stubbornness.

    Advanced Moves¶

    Move Description
    By Nature Sustained You don’t need to eat or drink. If a move tells you to mark off a ration, ignore it.
    Chosen One Requires: Cast a Divine Spell
    Choose one spell. You are granted that spell as if it was one level lower.
    Devoted Healer When you heal someone else of damage, add your level to the amount of damage healed.
    Divine Protection When you wear no armor or shield, you have 2 armor.
    Empower Requires Cast a Divine Spell
    When you cast a spell, on a 10+, you can choose from the 7-9 list. If you do, you may choose one of these effects as well:
    • The spell’s effects are doubled.
    • The spell’s targets are doubled.
    First Aid Requires Cast a Divine Spell
    Cure Light Wounds is a rote for you and therefore doesn’t count against your limit of granted spells.
    Iron Will When you are subject to mind control or magic that influences your feelings, you may choose to take d4 damage (ignores armor) and ignore the influence.
    Penitent When you take damage and embrace the pain, you may take +d4 damage (ignores armor). If you do, take +1 forward.
    Serenity You ignore -1 on casting for your first ongoing spell.
    Stigmata When you display your bleeding, take d4 damage (ignores armor) and choose one person who can see. They heal 2d8 damage. Your wounds leave bloody stains that do not come out until the damage caused by their infliction is healed.

    Master Moves¶

    Move Description
    Anointed Requires Chosen One
    Choose one spell in addition to the one you picked for the chosen one. You are granted that spell as if it was one level lower.
    Caretaker’s Insight When you Discern Realities, if you study someone who is afflicted, injured, or in pain, you may ask questions from this list instead:
    • What do you hope for the future?
    • What do you intend to do now?
    • What do you want from me?
    • What makes you feel beautiful or loved?
    • What’s wrong with you, and how could I help you?
    When you heal someone, also ask one of these questions.
    Divine Armor Replaces Divine Protection
    When you wear no armor or shield, you have 3 armor.
    Greater Empower Replaces Empower
    When you cast a spell, on a 10-11, you can choose from the 7-9 list. If you do, you may choose one of these effects as well. On a 12+, you can choose one of these effects for free.
    • The spell’s effects are doubled.
    • The spell’s targets are doubled.
    Greater First Aid Requires First Aid
    Cure Moderate Wounds is a rote for you and therefore doesn’t count against your limit of granted spells.
    Greater Stigmata Replaces Stigmata
    When you display your bleeding, take d4 damage (ignores armor) and choose one person who can see. They heal 2d8 damage and all your other allies who can see you heal d8 damage each. Your wounds leave bloody stains that do not come out until the damage caused by their infliction is healed.
    Healthy Distrust Whenever the unclean magic wielded by mortals causes you to Defy Danger, treat a miss as a 7-9 instead.
    Martyr Replaces Penitent
    When you take damage and embrace the pain, you may take +d4 damage (ignoring armor). If you do, gain +1 forward and add your level to any damage done or healed by your following action.
    Providence Requires Serenity
    You ignore -1 on casting for two ongoing spells.

    Spells¶

    Your faith in the Martyred Saint grants you spells in three of her divine spheres: healing, protection, and wisdom.

    Level Spell Description
    0 Endure Ongoing
    As long as this spell is ongoing, you or an ally you touch can endure extreme heat or cold, like walking across burning sands or swimming through arctic waters. While this spell is ongoing, take a -1 to cast a spell.
    0 Guidance The symbol of your deity appears before you and gestures towards the direction or course of action your deity would have you take, then disappears. The message is through gesture only; your communication through this spell is severely limited.
    0 Sanctify Your deity consecrates the food or water you hold in your hands while you cast this spell. In addition to being holy or unholy, the affected substance is purified of any mundane spoilage.
    1 Cure Light Wounds At your touch, wounds mend, and bones cease to ache. Heal an ally you touch of d8 damage.
    1 Detect Alignment When you cast this spell, choose an alignment: Follower of the Saint, Neutral to the Saint, Antithetical to the Saint. One of your senses is briefly able to detect that alignment. The GM will tell you what there is of that alignment.
    1 Invigorate One ally you touch is cured of fainting, lethargy, paralysis, or unconsciousness, and you can instantly move again.
    1 Mending Your devotion allows you to fix some mundane object without needing tools or expertise.
    1 Planning Ongoing
    Cast this spell when you devise a plan to understand the situation better. When you carry out that plan, gain +1 forward. You can only have a heightened understanding of one plan at a time.
    1 Sanctuary As you cast this spell, you walk the perimeter of an area, consecrating it to your deity. As long as you stay within that area, you are alerted whenever someone acts maliciously in the sanctuary (including entering with harmful intent). Anyone who receives healing within a sanctuary heals +d4 HP.
    1 Ward of Protection By drawing a simple glyph on one of your possessions, you will always know if and when it is taken from you and generally where it is located if that should happen. You may only have one Ward active at a time.
    3 Analgesia Ongoing
    You touch an ally for a second, and they are cured of all fear, nausea, and pain, whether mundane or magical. While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell.
    3 Cure Moderate Wounds You staunch bleeding and set bones through magic. Heal an ally you touch of 2d8 damage.
    3 Immunity Ongoing
    Name one specific source of harm, such as burning, cutting, lightning, or poisonous gas. While this spell is ongoing, you are immune to harm from that source and take a -1 to cast a spell. You may still be subject to secondary effects—a fire that does not burn you produces smoke that can still choke you, and drowning is uncomfortable.
    3 Inquiry Cast this spell on a person you can see and ask one question about them from the list below:
    • What does this person intend to do?
    • What does this person need or desire?
    • What is false about this person?
    • Who does this person associate with?
    Gain +1 forward when you act on the answer.
    3 Silence Ongoing
    Name a location you can see. While this spell is ongoing, no sound can be made in that place, and you take a -1 to cast a spell (assuming the Silence does not prevent spell casting outright).
    3 Local Customs Ongoing
    The customs of the people you observe are revealed to you. While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell, and you can determine and mimic local customs wherever you are. You can tell if someone’s behavior is routine or unexpected by observers, and you can predict what kind of behavior is expected of you. Still, you do not gain any additional language skills. While this spell is ongoing, take a -1 to cast a spell.
    5 Circle of Protection Ongoing
    Draw a circle on the ground around you or someone else. Until that person leaves the circle’s confines, no extra-planar entity can cross or extend its influence beyond it. While this spell is ongoing, take a -1 to cast a spell.
    5 Cure Critical Wounds Heal an ally you touch of 3d8 damage.
    5 Reverse Poison With a touch, you cure an ally of any ill effects caused by a poisonous substance, short of death itself. Damage inflicted by the poison itself is healed, but the damage caused by other sources enhanced by the poison remains.
    5 True Seeing Ongoing
    Your vision is open to the true nature of everything you focus on. You pierce illusions and see things that have been hidden. The GM will describe the area before you ignore any illusions and falsehoods, magical or otherwise. While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell.
    5 Words of the Unspeaking With a touch, you speak to the spirits within things. The non-living object you touch answers three questions you pose as best it can.
    7 Heal Touch an ally, and you may heal them of damage by as many points equal to your maximum HP.
    7 True Discernment When you cast this spell on the evidence left by some creature or event or something removed from far away, you may study it as if you had discerned realities and rolled 10+.
    7 Word of Recall Choose a word. After casting this spell, when you speak the chosen word, you and any allies touching you when you first cast the spell are immediately returned to where you cast the spell. You can only maintain a single location; casting Word of Recall again before speaking the word replaces the earlier spell.
    9 Divine Presence Ongoing
    Every creature must ask your leave to enter your presence, and you must give permission aloud for them to enter. Any creature without your leave takes an extra d10 damage whenever they take damage in your presence. While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell.
    9 Questing Describe an objective, however complicated. The GM will tell you what it would take to accomplish typically. You can choose one of those requirements, and the GM will tell you a more straightforward—and possible—way to achieve it.
    9 Repair Choose one event in the target’s past. All effects of that event, including damage, poison, disease, and magical effects, are ended and repaired. HP and diseases are healed, poisons are neutralized, and magical effects are ended.
