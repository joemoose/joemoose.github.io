    Monk of the Martyred Saint¶

    You are a fighter, but you fight with honor. For the followers of the Martyred Saint, you embody lawful Judgement. This is what you live and breathe as you wander, righting wrongs. In this domain, you work miracles.

    Stats¶

    Stat Value
    Base damage d6
    Load 9+STR
    Maximum HP 8+Constitution

    Diety¶

    You serve and worship the Martyred Saint. She speaks to and through you. She represents:

    • Mercy for the oppressed.
    • Wrath against slavery of body, mind, or soul, whether of the living or the dead.
    • The sanctity of suffering and is petitioned by such.

    The Martyred Saint has multiple personas manifest in different situations: Battle through her Templars; Healing, Protection, and Wisdom through her Disciples; Death through her Incarnations; Vengence through her Undying; And Judgement through you, her Monk.

    She has a favored group, a chosen people, the followers of the Saint.

    Heritage¶

    Heritage Move
    Dwarf As long as you have Chi left, you cannot be pushed, thrown, tripped, or knocked over.
    Elf As long as you have Chi left, you may defend with DEX instead of CON and hack and slash with DEX instead of STR.
    Halfling When you fight with honor, add this to the list of deeds you can spend a Chi to perform—Dodge a blow meant for you.
    Human When you arrive at a new settlement, followers of the Martyred Saint will accept you as their guest and provide you with friendship, hospitality, and advice. If you also agree to help them solve a problem they have, gain +1 forward.

    Drives¶

    • Advance the interests of the Martyred Saint.
    • Bring a villain to justice.
    • Choose honor over personal gain.
    • Fulfill a promise to protect someone during a dangerous journey or situation.
    • Ignore danger to aid another.

    Gear¶

    You have a token of membership in your honorable fighting society, such as a robe, sash, or scroll. Describe it.

    You have clothes appropriate to your calling and present situation, dungeon rations (5 uses, 1 weight), and choose one:

    • Adventuring gear (5 uses, 1 weight)
    • Bandages (3 uses, slow) and a healing potion

    Starting Moves¶

    Move Description
    Fight with Honour When you enter a fight, roll +CON.
    • On a 10+, hold 3 Chi.
    • On a 7-9, hold 2 Chi.
    • On a miss, hold 1 Chi anyway, but your enemies already have the drop on you.
    Spend a Chi during the fight to achieve one of the following effects:
    • Block a blow meant for you or someone else (you take half damage).
    • Break free from bonds or other confinement.
    • Cross the distance between yourself and a foe, bypassing all obstacles.
    • Deal your damage to a foe within reach.
    • Leap over or across a physical obstacle.
    You lose any remaining Chi once the fight is over.
    Fists of Fury You are never unarmed—your body and limbs are a weapon, just as good as any other.
    Invoke a Miracle Your faith and discipline to the teachings of the Martyred Saint allow you to invoke any miracle from her divine sphere of Judgment that does not exceed your level.

    When you invoke a judgement miracle, roll +WIS.
    • On a 10+, you cast the spell but choose one consequence from the list below.
      • The spell’s effect is doubled, but you may not invoke this miracle again until after you sleep for a night.
      • The spell’s targets are doubled, but you may not invoke this miracle again until after a night’s sleep.
      • The miracle has its typical results.
    • On a 7-9, you cast the miracle, but choose one:
      • You may only cast this spell again after you sleep for a night.
      • Your spellcasting is obvious, and you draw attention to yourself.

    Advanced Moves¶

    Move Description
    Arrows in Hand Requires Fists of Fury
    When you block or dodge a missile attack, roll+DEX.
    • On a 10+, you snatch the missile out of the air, and it deals no damage.
    • On a 7-9, choose one:
      • You avoid the attack completely.
      • You snatch the missile out of the air and take half the damage.
    Fuelled by Honour Requires Fight with Honour
    If you accomplish your drive during a fight, gain one Chi, up to a maximum of three.
    Hand of the Monkey Requires Fists of Fury
    If you are unarmoured and carry no shield, you have 2 armor against hand-held weapons. If this reduces the damage you take to zero, you may also disarm your foe.
    Honourable Society You are a respected member of an honorable fighting society. When you send word to them for aid, you may recruit as if you have a good reputation and are paying generously, even though you are not paying at all. Any hirelings they send to you have at least loyalty +1 and warrior +1.
    Light as a Feather When you run across a surface that cannot hold your weight, roll +DEX. On a 10+, you do it gracefully and without trouble. On a 7-9, you make it across, but with consequences. The GM will tell you what.

    Master Moves¶

    Move Description
    Glorious Honour Requires Fight with Honour
    When you fight with honor, hold an extra Chi, even on a miss.
    Hand of the Monkey King Replaces Hand of the Monkey
    If you are unarmoured and carry no shield, you have 4 armor against hand-held weapons.
    If this reduces the damage you take to zero, you may also disarm your foe.
    Superior Technique Requires Fight with Honour
    Add these to the list of deeds you can spend a chi to perform:
    • Break through a physical obstacle.
    • Deal your damage to a foe within sight.
    • Grab an item within reach. Now it is yours.
    Exacting Discipline When you invoke a miracle, on 12+, you can double the spell’s effect or the number of targets without losing the spell.

    Judgement Miracles¶

    Level Spell Description
    0 Contract When you sign a contract that you have cast this spell upon, you are mystically notified if the other party breaks it.
    1 Casting Stones Ongoing
    As long as you concentrate on Judgment, any stone you pick up becomes a powerful weapon (thrown, near, 1 piercing). While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell.
    1 Cause Fear Ongoing
    Choose a target you can see and a nearby object. The target is afraid of the object so long as you maintain the spell. The target’s reaction is up to the GM: flee, panic, beg, fight. While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell. You cannot target entities with less than animal intelligence (magical constructs, undead, automatons, and the like).
    3 Dispel Magic Choose a spell or magic effect in your presence; this spell rips it apart. Lesser spells are ended, and powerful magic is reduced or dampened as long as you are nearby.
    3 Hold Person Ongoing
    Choose a person you can see. They can only act if you cast a spell or leave their presence. This effect ends immediately if the target takes damage from any source.
    5 Contagion Ongoing
    Choose a creature you can see. Until you end this spell, the target suffers from a disease of your choice. While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell.
    7 Harm Touch an enemy and strike them with divine wrath—deal 2d8 damage and d6 damage to yourself. This damage ignores armor.
    9 Plague Ongoing
    Name a city, town, encampment, or other place people live. As long as this spell is active, that place is beset by a plague appropriate to your deity’s domains (locusts, death of the firstborn, etc.). While this spell is ongoing, you take a -1 to cast a spell.
