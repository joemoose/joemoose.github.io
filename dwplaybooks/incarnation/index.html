    Incarnation of the Martyred Saint¶

    You are a living reincarnation of the Martyred Saint—The One Who Endures. You carry on her battle against necromancy and foul sorcery.

    Stats¶

    • Base damage: d6
    • Load: 10+STR
    • Maximum HP: 8+Constitution

    Diety¶

    You serve and worship the Martyred Saint. She speaks to and through you. She represents:

    • Mercy for the oppressed.
    • Wrath against slavery of body, mind, or soul, whether living or dead.
    • The sanctity of suffering and is petitioned by such.

    The Martyred Saint has multiple personas manifest in different situations: Battle through her Templars; Healing, Protection, and Wisdom through her Disciples; Judgement through her Monks; Vengence through her Undying; And Death through you, her Incarnation.

    She has a favored group, a chosen people, her followers of the Saint.

    Heritage¶

    • Dwarf: Choose one of the following:

      • When you deal damage to the undead, deal +1d4 damage.
      • When you swear an oath according to the precepts of your faith, take +1 forward to accomplish it. If you fail to do so, or turn away from your oath (to swear another one, for example), take -1 forward.

    • Elf: Choose one of the following:

      • When you Discern Reality, on a hit, your keen senses also tell you if there are any spirits, ghosts, undead, or extra-planar entities here.
      • Gain +1 forward after meditating for a day.

    • Halfling: Choose one of the following:

      • When you Spout Lore concerning the domain of death or necromancy, take +1 forward.
      • You sing the healing songs of spring and brook. When you make camp, you and your allies heal +d6.

    • Human: Choose one of the following:

      • When you touch someone, you can always tell if they are possessed, and if so, by what.
      • When you turn the undead, you may also ask one question about them, as if you had Discerned Reality and rolled a 7-9.

    Example Drives¶

    • Bring the benefits of your deity to people of a different faith.
    • Endanger yourself to follow the precepts you are devoted to.
    • Punish a necromancer or summoner of spirits for their misdeeds.
    • Free someone from a troublesome spirit.

    Gear¶

    You have clothes appropriate to your calling and present situation, dungeon rations (5 uses, 1 weight), and an item that symbolizes your devotion (0 weight, describe it). Choose two:

    • Adventuring gear (5 uses, 1 weight).
    • Bag of books (5 uses, 2 weight).
    • Bandages (3 uses, slow) and a healing potion.
    • Chainmail (1 armor, 1 weight).
    • Mace or warhammer (close, 1 weight).
    • Shield (+1 armor, 2 weight).
    • Staff (close, two-handed, 1 weight).

    Starting Moves¶

    You gain the following moves at level one.

    Circle of Protection¶

    When you draw a magical circle on the ground, roll +WIS. On a 10+, it will keep spirits, ghosts, or extra-planar entities in or out until someone else breaks it. On a 7-9, it will keep them in or out momentarily, but only briefly.

    Divine Guidance¶

    When you petition your deity according to the precepts of your Martyred Saint, you are granted some helpful knowledge or boon related to your deity’s domain. The GM will tell you what.

    Ghost Seer¶

    When you invoke your faith by prayer, you can interact with ghostly, insubstantial, or extra-planar entities, including damaging them if your faith opposes them. However, you can’t deal with the entity separately if they possess someone.

    A Helping Hand¶

    When you enter a civilized settlement, you can always find a follower of the Martyred Saint who will take you in as an honored guest.

    Turn Undead¶

    When you hold your holy symbol aloft and call on your devotion for protection, roll +WIS. On a hit, so long as you continue to pray and brandish your holy symbol, no undead may come within reach of you. On a 10+, you also momentarily daze intelligent undead and cause mindless undead to flee.

    Aggression breaks the effects, and they can act as expected. Intelligent undead may still find ways to harry you from afar. They’re clever like that.

    Advanced Moves¶

    You may pick from the following moves starting at level two.

    Abjuration¶

    When you banish a creature from another plane of existence, roll +INT. On a 10+, it may not approach you further; if it lacks an anchor in this world, it must return to whence it came. On a 7-9, it may only approach you further if you allow it to (or let your guard down). On a miss, you cannot banish it.

    Ashes to Ashes¶

    Requires Turn Undead

    The first time you brandish your holy symbol at an undead creature, deal your damage to it (ignores armor).

    Divine Intervention¶

    Requires Commune or Divine Guidance

    When you petition the Martyred Saint, suffer a debility, and you gain one protection. Lose any protection you already held from this move. Spend that protection when you or an ally take damage to call on your deity, who intervenes with an appropriate manifestation and negates the damage.

    Ghost Killer¶

    When you attack a spirit, ghost, or extra-planar entity, your damage die is a d10.

    Ghost Talker¶

    You can see and interact with all ghosts and spirits of the dead and peer into the spiritual workings of the undead, even if they are mindless. When studying an undead creature, ask whatever questions you like from the Discern Reality list.

    Heaven’s Weaving¶

    When you Parley* with a character, your faith grants you insight into them. Ask their player two questions from the *Discern Reality list.

    Orison for Guidance¶

    Requires Personal Deity

    When you sacrifice something of value to your deity and pray for guidance, your deity tells you what it would have you do. If you do it, mark XP.

    Spiritual Connection¶

    If you have an item from someone that they highly value, you can interact with them as if they were a spirit, ghost, or extra-planar entity and use moves against them.

    The Scales of Life and Death¶

    When someone takes their last breath in your presence, they take +1 to their roll.

    To Know the Unknowable¶

    When you encounter a creature, manifestation, or power from another plane of existence, you can ask the GM from whence it came and what its properties are. Take +1 forward when acting on the answers.

    Master Moves¶

    You may pick from the following moves starting at level six.

    Armor of Faith¶

    Requires Turn Undead

    While turning undead, you and nearby allies are protected, gaining +2 armor. This bonus lasts only as long as there are undead present.

    Ars Goetia¶

    When you first encounter an extra-planar entity, you may ask the GM any one question. The GM will answer truthfully.

    Funeral Rites¶

    When you take time after a battle to dedicate your victory to the Martyred Saint’s ideals and bury the dead, take +1 forward.

    Cheat Death Again¶

    When you have zero HP, rather than taking your last breath, roll +WIS. On a 10+, you live and choose one:

    • You discover one of Death’s dark secrets.
    • You have a moment with a soul that has already passed on.
    • You manage to avoid Death’s notice.

    On a 7-9, you live, but Death takes notice, and you discover something terrible. On a miss, you can cheat death no longer, and you must pass through the Black Gate.

    Closing the Gateway¶

    When you attack a spirit, ghost, or extra-planar entity, you deal +d6 damage, and your foe can choose to return from whence it came instead of taking damage.

    When you reduce a spirit, ghost, or extra-planar creature to zero HP, its soul is subsequently barred from appearing on the material plane in any form. It can still be encountered in astral visions and other planes, but not in your world.

    Command of Injunction¶

    When you command someone in your presence to cease a specific activity, roll +WIS. On a 10+, they must comply to the best of their ability. On a 7-9, they must comply until you leave their presence.

    Only one injunction you issue can stand at any time. Only activities consciously undertaken can be subject to injunction.

    Divine Invincibility¶

    Replaces Divine Intervention

    When you petition your deity, suffer a debility, and you gain 2 protection. Lose any protection you already held. Spend one protection when you or an ally takes damage to call on your deity, who intervenes with an appropriate manifestation and negates the damage.

    Dust to Dust¶

    Requires Ashes to Ashes

    When you reduce a creature to zero HP, its soul is put to rest and cannot be raised again as an undead creature.

    Evidence of Faith¶

    When you see divine magic as it happens, you can ask the GM which deity granted the spell and its effects. Take +1 forward when acting on the answers.
