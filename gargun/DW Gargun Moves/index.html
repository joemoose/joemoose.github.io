    Moves

    Gargûn Player Character Basic Moves

    The following moves are available to all gargûn player characters.

    Attack!

    When you try to injure someone, describe how you attack them:

    • If you do it up close with clubs, knives, or bites, roll +STR.
    • If you do it at a distance with spears, rocks, or excrement, roll +DEX.

    On a 10+, your attack connects, and you deal your damage. On a 7-9, your attack connects, but! You are off-balance and exposed. Deal one-half of your total damage. The GM will tell you who takes advantage of your position.

    Run Away!

    When you try to avoid immediate danger, describe how:

    • Through a surprising burst of strength, roll +STR.
    • By moving quickly to get out of the way, roll +DEX.
    • By taking it on the chin, roll +CON.

    On a 10+, you do what you set out to do, and the threat doesn't come to bear. On a 7-9, you've bollixed it up. The GM will give you a hard bargain, a worse outcome, or an ugly choice.

    Lie Through Your Fangs

    When you try to BS or fast talk yourself out of a dire situation without offering anything in return, roll +INT. On a 10+, you can pass off your lie and calm the situation. On a 7-9, you can pass off your lie, but! You have to give something up or expose yourself to danger to put the lie over.

    Suss It Out

    When you try to figure out what the hell is going on, roll +WIS. On a 10+, ask the GM two questions from the following list. On a 7–9, ask one.

    Either way, take +1 forward when acting on the answers.

    • What happened here recently?
    • What is about to try and kill us, besides everything?
    • What can we see that others would miss?
    • How can I escape?
    • Where is the shiny?

    Let's Make a Deal

    When you attempt to plead your case to non-gargûn, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they listen long enough for you to offer them a deal; you can't always get what you want, but if you try sometimes, you just might find you get what you need. On a 7-9, they're openly aggressive, and you'll need to sweeten the deal for them to even consider what you're suggesting.

    Abet or Thwart

    When you help or hinder another gargûn, roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, you apply either a +1 or a -2 to the roll, your choice. On a 7–9, as a roll of 10+, but! You expose yourself to danger, retribution, or cost.

    Tribal Knowledge

    When you explain the unknown by retelling the stories of the Tribe, roll +Ingenuity. On a 10+, a detailed story explains precisely this situation; tell it. Take +1 forward when acting on this information. On a 7-9, there are some hints or vague legends; what are they?

    The Gargûn Tribe Moves

    The following Moves are available only to the Gargûn Tribe character shared by the players.

    Tribe Basic Moves

    The Gargûn Tribe starts with the following moves.

    Round Up

    When you recruit additional gargûn to help with a mission, give an inspiring speech and roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, you gain two Warbands of gargûn. On a 7-9, you gain one Warband of gargûn. On a miss, the Tribe's lack of faith in you is demoralizing. All gargûn player characters take -2 forward.

    Note: When you take Warbands from the Tribe, reduce your population score respectively—one Warband equals one population—until you bring those Warbands back to the Tribe.

    Run and Hide!

    When the Tribe is raided by murderous outsiders, roll +Secrecy. On a 10+, the raiders couldn't find any of your Tribe; no population is lost. On a 7-9, the raiders found some of your Tribe but didn’t find your lair! Subtract d6 from your Tribe's population. On a miss, the murder hobos found your lair and slaughtered your Tribe; loose 2d6 population.

    Stockpiles

    When a gargûn character returns from a mission and hoards the resources obtained (instead of sharing them), roll +Ingenuity. On a 10+, the Tribe gains 3 stockpiles. On a 7-9, the Tribe gains 1 stockpile.

    Note: Stockpiles persist between sessions. Spend one stockpile at any time to move a Want progress clock back one tick.

    Zug Zug!

    When a gargûn player character returns from a mission and shares the resources obtained (instead of hoarding), your Tribe enters a breeding frenzy. Reset an appropriate Want clock and roll +Hardiness. On a 10+, add d6 to your population. On a 7-9, add d6 to your population, but add a new Want. On a miss, the distraction of the breeding season has opened you up to disaster! The GM immediately brings an external pressure to bear on the Tribe.

    Tribe Advanced Moves

    The following Advanced Tribe Moves cost 7 XP each to acquire.

    A Bit of the Ultra-Violence

    Your gargûn have grown up extra bloodthirsty! They deal an additional d4 damage when attacking up close.

    All for One!

    Any number of gargûn player characters may join together as a Warband during battle. The characters now attack as a single Warband dealing d12 damage + number joined.

    Any damage taken by the Warband is spread amongst the gargûn player characters in the Warband.

    The Warband is dissolved after either three rounds of combat, or a player character falls.

    Bounty of the Wild

    By scavenging, your Tribe can alleviate a Want. Whenever you reset a Want clock, roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, reduce another Want clock by three ticks. On a 7-9, reduce another Want clock by one tick.

    Huddle Up

    When you pack together for defence during combat, roll +Warfare. On a 10+, all gargûn involved gain +1 armour for the rest of combat. On a 7-9, all gargûn involved gain +1 armour until a gargûn falls.

    Fuck You, We Have a…

    Your Tribe gains a mighty protector! Of course, they want something in return.

    …Wizard!

    A mysterious wizard has taken up residence in your lair. Gain four homunculi Warbands (d6 damage, 10 HP).

    …Troll!

    A mighty troll has taken up residence in your lair. Gain one troll Warband (d12 damage, 20 HP, regenerates one hit point per round).

    …Necromancer!

    A foul necromancer has taken up residence in your lair. Gain five skeleton Warbands (d6 damage, 8 HP, mindless). Gain an additional skeleton Warband each time your village breeds.

    …Drake!

    A legendary drake (or is it a dragon?) has taken up residence in your lair! Gain one drake Warband (2d8 damage, 40 HP, terrifying).

    Notes:

    • This move can be taken multiple times, but only in the listed order.
    • These sorts of creatures aren’t known for sharing. Each time a new protector moves into your lair, they run off the previous protector. You can only have one protector at a time.
    • You may spend additional XP to skip a protector level. For example, spend 14 XP to skip the Wizard and go straight to Troll when you first select this move.
    • Each protector demands some new Want from the Tribe. Describe specifically what it is and how to obtain it.
    • If a protector dies, its Warband is gone forever. You may, however, still acquire the next level protector.
    • When a protector leaves the Tribe for any reason, their specific Want goes with them. You don’t have to find more rotten fish to feed your troll after it's killed by a murder hobo.

    Gargûn Go Boom!

    You now can recruit Gargûn Sapper Warbands in addition to regular Gargûn Warbands.

    These suicidal saps deal d4 +Warfare damage and have 4 +Hardiness HP and a peculiar EXPLOSIVE! move.

    Any gargûn character can trigger the sapper's EXPLOSIVE! move: the sapper Warband deals 2d8 damage to all nearby Warbands, including allies, and is destroyed.

    Gargûn Riders

    Your gargûn are now mounted warriors! Describe what you ride and how the Tribe domesticated these beasts. Your gargûn character always succeeds if you try to escape combat. Add a new external pressure to describe a predator that hunts your mounts.

    Gargûn Sawbones

    Your Tribe has passed down a few jars of leeches from generation to generation. There are now healers (kinda) in your Tribe.

    When you return to your lair after a mission and seek healing, roll +Ingenuity. On a 10+, the leeches worked! Recover all lost HP. On a 7-9, perhaps a little too much bloodletting, recover one-half of your lost HP. On a miss, well, they didn't think you needed that organ, take d4 damage.

    Hardy Gargûn

    Your Tribe is more stalwart than typical gargûn thanks to a cruel Spartan upbringing. They roll 2d8 (instead of 1d6), keeping the highest result for their hit points. All active gargûn player characters may re-roll their hit points when this move is initially taken.

    Lair Fortifications

    Your Tribe has improved the lair's defences. Describe how. Whenever your Tribe is raided, roll your population loss twice and take the lower result.

    Lair Improvements

    Increase a single Tribe stat by +1 to a maximum of +3. Describe how the Tribe improves the lair.

    Master Tribe Moves

    The following Master Tribe Moves cost 14 XP each to aquire.

    Note: Any moves marked with * become a basic move for all individual gargûn player characters.

    Boffins

    Gargûn player characters gain a new ability. Each subsequent generation gains 2 +WIS abilities rather than 1 +WIS.

    Gargosians

    The Tribe is now able to share simple thoughts and feelings. Whenever a gargûn character is in the company of another gargûn and would roll +INT, you may now roll +Ingenuity instead. This ability has a distinct visual effect. Explain what it is and how others react to it.

    Gargûn Angry!*

    When you unleash your inner gargûn rage and charge an enemy Warband, roll +Warfare. On a 10+, your anger is ferocious! Gain +2 to STR and -1 to CON for five rounds. On a 7-9, it seems like you’re kinda upset but not really feeling it today. Gain +1 to STR and -2 to CON for five rounds, and you cannot unleash your rage again until the next mission.

    Loc-Nar

    You’ve unearthed a magical totem in your lair. What the heck is it? All gargûn player characters immediately gain +1 to any single stat.

    Some Words Have Power*

    Thoughts have a certain sound. That being the equivalent to a form. Through sound and motion, you would be able to paralyze nerves, shatter bones, set fires, suffocate an enemy or burst his organs. Or something like that. Probably just the paralyze thing.

    When you speak the power word, roll +Hardiness. On a 10+, you paralyze your target. On a 7-9, you paralyze your target and choose two of the following:

    • You draw unwanted attention.
    • You take d4 damage from the strain of the word.
    • You are knocked back and fall prone.

    Spot Me, Bro

    Your Tribe has learned to work well together when attempting feats of strength, thanks to excellent workout routines by the elders. Whenever a gargûn character is in the company of another gargûn and would roll +STR, you may now roll +Warfare instead.

    Ten Mouths are Better Than One

    Your Tribe has learned to play off each other’s lies and grovelling, making you all more convincing and pathetic as a group. Whenever a gargûn character is in the company of another gargûn and would roll +CHR, you may now roll +Teamwork instead.

    Wondergargu Powers Activate!*

    When you enlist a friendly Warband to aid you in your actions, roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, take +3 forward as the Warband comes to your aid. On a 7–9, take +1 forward, but the boneheads suffer d4 damage helping you.
