    Moves

    Individual Gargûn Characters Basic Moves

    Attack!

    When you try to injure someone, describe how you attack them:

    • If you do it up close with clubs, knives, or bites, roll +STR.
    • If you do it at a distance with spears or rocks, roll +DEX.

    On a 10+, your attack connects, and you deal your damage. On a 7-9, your attack connects, but! You are off-balance and exposed. Deal ½ your damage rounded down. The GM will tell you who takes advantage of your position.

    Run Away!

    When you avoid immediate danger, describe how:

    • Through a surprising burst of strength, roll +STR.
    • By moving quickly to duck out of the way, roll +DEX.
    • By taking it on the chin, roll +CON.

    On a 10+, you do what you set out to do, and the threat doesn't come to bear. On a 7-9, you've bollixed it up. The GM will give you a hard bargain, a worse outcome, or an ugly choice.

    Lie Through Your Fangs

    When you try to BS or fast talk yourself out of a dire situation without offering anything in return, roll +INT. On a 10+, you can pass off your lie. On a 7-9, you can pass off your lie, but! You have to give something up or expose yourself to danger to put the lie over.

    Suss It Out

    When you try to figure out what the hell is going on, roll +WIS. On a 10+, ask the GM two questions from the following list. On a 7–9, ask one.

    Either way, take +1 forward when acting on the answers.

    • What happened here recently?
    • What is about to try and kill us, besides everything?
    • What can we see that others would miss?
    • How can I escape?
    • Where is the shiny?

    Let's Make a Deal

    When you attempt to plead your case to non-gargûn, roll +CHA. On a 10+, they listen long enough for you to offer them a deal; you can't always get what you want, but if you try sometimes, you just might find you get what you need. On a 7-9, they're openly aggressive, and you'll need to sweeten the deal for them to even consider what you're offering.

    Abet or Thwart

    When you help or hinder another gargûn, roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, you apply either a +1 or a -2 to the roll, your choice. On a 7–9, as a roll of 10+, but! You expose yourself to danger, retribution, or cost.

    Tribal Knowledge

    When you explain the unknown by retelling the stories of your tribe, roll +Ingenuity. On a 10+, a detailed story explains precisely this situation; tell it. Take +1 forward when acting on this information. On a 7-9, there are some hints or vague legends; what are they?

    The Tribe Moves

    Tribe Starting Moves

    Round Up

    When you recruit additional gargûn to help with a task, give an inspiring speech and roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, you gain two Warbands of gargûn. On a 7-9, you gain one Warband of gargûn. On a miss, the tribe's lack of faith in you is demoralizing. All gargûn characters take -2 forward.

    Note: When you take Warbands from the Tribe, reduce your population respectively—one Warband equals one population—until you bring those Warbands back to the tribe.

    Run and Hide!

    When your tribe is raided by murderous outsiders, roll +Secrecy. On a 10+, the raiders couldn't find any of your tribe; no population is lost. On a 7-9, the raiders found some of your tribe but didn’t find your lair! Subtract d6 from your tribe's population. On a miss, the murder hobos found your lair and slaughtered your tribe; loose 2d6 population.

    Zug Zug!

    When a gargûn character returns from a mission and shares the vital resources obtained, your tribe enters a breeding frenzy. Reset an appropriate Want clock and roll +Hardiness. On a 10+, add d6 to your population. On a 7-9, add d6 to your population, but add a new Want. On a miss, the distraction of the breeding season has opened you up to disaster! The GM immediately brings an external pressure to bear on the tribe.

    Stockpiles

    When a gargûn character returns from a mission and hoards the vital resources obtained instead of sharing them, roll +Ingenuity. On a 10+, the tribe gains three stockpile hold. On a 7-9, the tribe gains two stockpile hold.

    Spend one hold at anytime to move a Want progress clock back one tick.

    Tribe Advanced Moves

    A Bit of the Ultra-Violence

    Your gargûn have grown up extra violent! They deal an additional d4 damage when attacking up close in melee.

    Huddle Together

    When you pack together for defence during combat, roll +Warfare. On a 10+, all gargûn involved gain +1 armour for the rest of combat. On a 7-9, all gargûn involved gain +1 armour until a gargûn falls.

    Bounty of the Wild

    By scavenging, your tribe can alleviate a Want. Whenever you reset a Want clock, roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, reduce another Want clock by two ticks. On a 7-9, reduce another Want clock by one tick.

    Fuck You, We Have a...

    Your village gains a mighty protector! Of course, they want something in return.

    ...Wizard!

    A mysterious wizard has taken up residence in your lair. Gain four homunculi Warbands (d6 damage, 10 HP).

    ...Troll!

    A mighty troll has taken up residence in your lair. Gain one troll Warband (d12 damage, 20 hit point, regenerates one hit points per round).

    ...Necromancer!

    A foul necromancer has taken up residence in your lair. Gain five skeleton Warbands (d6 damage, 8 hit point, mindless). Gain an additional skeleton Warband each time your village breeds.

    ...Drake!

    A legendary drake (or is it a dragon?) has taken up residence in your lair! Gain one drake Warband (2d8 damage, 40 HP, terrifying).

    Notes:

    • This move can be taken multiple times, but only in the listed order. These sorts of creatures aren’t known for sharing. Each time a new protector moves into your lair, they run off the previous protector. Thus, you can only have one protector at a time.
    • You may spend additional XP to skip protector levels. For example, spend 14 XP to skip the Wizard and go straight to Troll when you first select this move.
    • Each protector demands some new Want from the tribe. Describe specifically what it is and how to obtain it.
    • If a protector dies, its Warband is gone forever. You may, however, still acquire the next level protector.
    • When a protector leaves the tribe for any reason, their specific Want goes with them. You don’t have to find more rotten fish to feed your troll protector after it's killed by a murder hobo.

    Hardy Gargûn

    Your gargûn characters are tougher than most gargûn. They roll 2d8 (instead of d6) keeping the highest d8 for their hit points. All active gargûn characters may re-roll their hit points when this move is initially taken.

    Gargûn Sawbones

    The tribe has passed down a few medicinal remedies and jars of leeches from generation to generation. There are now healers in your tribe.

    When you return to your lair after a mission and seek healing, roll +Ingenuity. On a 10+, the leeches worked! Recover all lost HP and debilities. On a 7-9, perhaps a little too much bloodletting, recover ½ lost HP and debilities. On a miss, well, they didn't think you needed that organ, take d4 damage.

    Gargûn Riders

    Your gargûn are now mounted warriors! Describe what your gargûn ride and how they domesticated these beasts. Your gargûn character always succeed when you try to escape combat. Add an external pressure to describe a predator that hunts on your mounts.

    Sapper Warbands

    You now can recruit gargûn sapper Warbands as well as regular gargûn Warbands. These suicidal Warbands deal d6 damage and have 8 HP as well as the move EXPLOSIVE. Any gargûn character can trigger the sapper's EXPLOSIVE move: the sapper Warband deals 2d6 damage to all nearby Warbands, including allies, and is destroyed.

    All for One!

    Any number of gargûn characters may join together as a Warband during a battle. The gargûn now attack as a Warband dealing d12 damage + the total number joined. Any damage taken by the Warband is spread evenly amongst the gargûn characters in the Warband. The Warband is dissolved after three rounds or as soon as one of the gargûn characters fall.

    Lair Fortifications

    The tribe has improved the defences of your lair. Describe how. Whenever your tribe is raided, roll your population loss twice and take the lower result.

    Lair Improvements

    Increase a single tribe stat by +1 to a maximum of +3. Describe how the tribe improves the lair.

    Loc-Nar

    You’ve unearthed a magical totem in your lair. What is it? All gargûn characters immediately gain +1 to any single stat.

    Master Tribe Moves

    The following Master Tribe Moves cost 14 XP. Any marked * becomes a basic move for all gargûn characters.

    Boffins

    Gargûn characters gain a new ability. Each subsequent generation gains 2 +WIS abilities rather than 1 +WIS.

    Gargosians

    The tribe is now able to share simple thoughts and feelings. Whenever a gargûn character is in the company of other gargûn and would roll +INT, they may now roll +Ingenuity instead. This ability has a distinct visual effect. Explain what it is and how others react to it.

    Gargûn Angry!*

    When you unleash your inner gargûn rage and charge an enemy Warband, roll +Warfare. On a 10+, your anger is ferocious! Gain +2 to STR and -1 to CON for five rounds. On a 7-9, it seems like you’re kinda upset but not really feeling it today. Gain +1 to STR and -2 to CON for five rounds, and you cannot unleash your rage again until the next mission.

    Some Words Have Power*

    Thoughts have a certain sound. That being the equivalent to a form. Through sound and motion, you would be able to paralyze nerves, shatter bones, set fires, suffocate an enemy or burst his organs. Or something like that. Probably just the paralyze thing.

    When you speak the power word, roll +Hardiness. On a 10+, you paralyze your target. On a 7-9, you paralyze your target and choose two of the following:

    • You draw unwanted attention.
    • You take d4 damage from the strain of the word.
    • You are knocked back and fall prone.

    Spot Me, Bro

    The tribe has learned to work well together when attempting feats of strength, thanks to excellent workout routines by the elders. Whenever a gargûn character is in the company of other gargûn and would roll +STR, they may now roll +Warfare instead.

    Ten Mouths are Better Than One

    The tribe has learned to play off each other’s lies and grovelling, making you all more convincing as a group. Whenever a gargûn character is in the company of other gargûn and would roll +CHR, they may now roll +Teamwork instead.

    Wondergargu Powers Activate!*

    When you enlist a friendly Warband to aid you in your actions, roll +Teamwork. On a 10+, take +3 forward as the Warband comes to your aid. On a 7–9, take +1 forward, but the boneheads suffer d4 damage.
